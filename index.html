about me

Hello! Thanks for visiting my website. This site is the culmination of about a year long project to learn web development. Built with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Jquery, and Semantic UI, I think this website expresses my love for building purposeful products that are fun to use.

I am currently a 4th year Mechanical Engineering student in a 5 year program at the University of Cincinnati. Through my college tenure I have worked at GE Appliances for three co-op rotations and the FirstBuild Innovation Center and Microfactory for one rotation. I have worked on projects implementing solutions onto production lines, developing mechanical solutions for heat flow and fluid flow problems, as well as working cross-functionally to develop electro-mechanical devices like an adaptive bass drum pedal.

Outside of work and school, I previously spent 2.5 years working with Engineers Without Borders developing a clean water system with a community in northwest Tanzania. I also served as a President of UC Hunger Awareness, a UC student group where I led a strategic initiative to build resources for students with food insecurities including a food pantry software and a cookbook.

At FirstBuild, I had the opportunity to work on the Forge NPI Program designing an innovative ice maker for whisky and bourbon enthusiasts. Due to the great quality our consumers expect in their drinks, we challenged ourselves to provide an equal great quality product to accompany.

GE Appliances

My first co-op rotation was with the Range Cost Out Program. Right off the bat, I managed 3 projects that encompassed $3 million of potential cost savings.

While rapid iterating designs for one of the projects, I noticed that our current data collection method caused us to miss a lot of information. To solve this, I learned how to write macros in Visual Basic for Applications (VBA), and built a macro with a simple user interface that created a heat map of user selected data. This greatly improved the amount of data we were able to analyze and process.
